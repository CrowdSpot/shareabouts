Frequently Asked Questions

1. How long is the map open for?

The map will be open for your contributions until Friday 8 May, 2015.

2. What happens to the data collected?

The results and data from this map and survey will be used to inform the City of Melbourne's draft Bicycle Plan 2016-2020. The strategy will identify opportunities and strategies to facilitate better bicycle facilities throughout the city. Your contribution will help ensure that the plan helps meets the needs of the local community.

3. Do you delete any contributions?

All spots are subject to moderation from time to time to check for inappropriate content. For more information, please view the City of Melbourne's Moderation Policy.

4. What if I make a mistake or want to change something?

If you need to change something, email info@crowdspot.com.aud we'll help sort it out.

5. How will my personal information be used?

Your privacy is important. The information is held in accordance with our Privacy Policy.

6. Where can I put my spot?

A spot can be placed anywhere within the City of Melbourne boundary and approximately 500 metres outside the boundary so you can comment on any ideas or issues for connecting routes.

6. What are the routes contained in the legend?

Off-road routes are bicycle paths that are separate to roads. These are often shared with walkers. Some are for bikes only such as along Harbour Esplanade, Docklands.

On-road bike lanes are routes on existing roads with some form of signage and support for riders. These can vary from fully separated lanes such as on LaTrobe Street, to lanes with only line marking.

Informal bike routes are roads commonly used by riders. There is limited formal support for riders but riders may choose the route because it is a low speed or quiet environment.

These routes appear on the TravelSmart map 2014. A full review of mapping will be conducted as part of the bicycle planning process.

7. What does bike parking relate in the legend?

A small bicycle icon (when zoomed-in) denotes where every City of Melbourne bike hoop is located. There are over 2000 bicycle hoops in the municipality.
