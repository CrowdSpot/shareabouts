Frequently asked questions

1. What happens to the information that is collected?

The information you provide about Plenty Gorge Park – what you like and dislike, the activities you do here and your ideas to improve the park – will shape a Master Plan for the next 10 years of the park’s development. Specifically, your comments will help Parks Victoria to understand the types of activities they need to plan for at Plenty Gorge and help them to prioritise improvements to the park.

If you provide an email address with your spot, we will send you further information about the Master Plan process to keep you in the loop.

2. What if I make a mistake or want to change some details?

If you need to change something, email Anthony (anthony@crowdspot.com.au) and he'll help sort it out.

3. Do you delete any contributions?

All spots are subject to moderation from time to time to check for relevance (spots may be edited or omitted). This is a space for people to engage in discussion and we expect all contributors to be respectful. Comments which include inappropriate language or personal abuse will be removed.

Your spot will appear immediately so play nice!

4. How long is the map open for?

At this stage, we expect the map to be open until 30 July 2014.

5. What types of things should I provide input about?

We’re interested to hear about all aspects of the park, tell us:

  • Which sections, paths, features, facilities, views, wildlife and plant life you like or dislike

  • The activities you like to do here, from picnicking, walking and camping to games and birdwatching

  • Your great idea for something new or something that could be improved

    • These are just ideas though. Perhaps think of your last visit to Plenty Gorge and go from there!

    6. What are the stages in developing the Plenty Gorge Park Master Plan?

    The Plenty Gorge Park Master Plan is being developed in a number of stages, these are proposed as:

  • DISCOVER (June – July 2014): a range of activities are being undertaken to gather ideas and input with the community and specific stakeholders interested in the park.

  • REVIEW (Late 2014): Drawing on all the input provided from the ‘discover’ consultation phase, a draft Master Plan will be provided for your review and feedback.

  • FINALISE (Late 2014 / Early 2015): The final Master Plan will be provided to government and following endorsement, will be released.

  • IMPLEMENT: The Plenty Gorge Master Plan will shape how the park is used, developed and improved over the next 10 years.
