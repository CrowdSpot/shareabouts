Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is public space?

Public space in the City of Port Phillip refers to freely accessible outdoor places which provide opportunities for all to connect, enjoy, recreate, and feel safe.

Council is moving beyond the traditional notion of parks and gardens providing this function and including the broader public space network to ensure we have sufficient opportunities for our community.

2. What does public space include?

Such spaces include the foreshore, parks, gardens, reserves, waterways, squares, streets and laneways. Public space also refers to shared public/private space not owned or managed by Council, such as Albert Park, school grounds, rooftops, green walls and mid-level spaces.

3. Why is public space important?

Research has also shown that public spaces provide:

  • positive economic value to an area;
  • positive impacts on physical and mental health;
  • benefits for children and young people;
  • potential to reduce crime and fear of crime;
  • opportunities for social connection;
  • value from biodiversity and nature;

4. Why do we need a strategy?

The strategy will provide a framework for all decision making regarding the future expansion, maintenance and upgrade of the public space network in the City of Port Phillip. It will consider methods to future proof our city and develop engaged, healthy, resilient and vibrant communities based on our planning considerations and the main drivers for change.

The results and data of this project will:

  • Inform site visits, walking tours and other Congress activities
  • Be a real time spatial resource for all delegates to Congress and for members when they visit Melbourne
