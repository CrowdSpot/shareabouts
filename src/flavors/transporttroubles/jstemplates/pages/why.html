Frequently Asked Questions

1. What are the different spot types and colours?

Blue lines and spots are trains. Orange lines and spots are buses. Green lines and spots are trams. Yellow spots are ‘like spots’ to indicate where something worked well.

2. What if I make a mistake or want to change something?

If you need to change something, email transporttroubles@vic.greens.org.au for assistance.

3. Do you delete any contributions?

All spots are subject to moderation from time to time to check for inappropriate content (spots may be edited or omitted). To keep things usable, older data may be removed from the system as more transport troubles are lodged.

4. What happens to the data collected?

All the information collected will be used to help the Victorian Greens better understand community views about their experiences on public transport – what’s working, and what needs to be improved. The data is helpful in mapping out Greens policies for public transport investment and improvements. Using this data, we’ll lobby Daniel Andrews and the Labor Government to fix what you’ve said is broken.

5. How will my personal information be used?

Your privacy is important. The information being requested is collected by CrowdSpot and will be used and held in accordance with CrowdSpot’s Privacy Policy and the Australian Greens Privacy Policy. The information may be used for the purpose of providing feedback on the results of this project, keeping in touch with you on our latest news, upcoming projects and/or events. If you do not provide personal information, we may not be able to provide you with updates or information. If you do not want to receive updates or to access or change your information, please contact us at transporttroubles@vic.greens.org.au

6. What is CrowdSpot?

The Victorian Greens are using CrowdSpot to run the #TransportTroubles campaign. CrowdSpot is an interactive, visual, map-based tool carefully designed to generate greater levels of participation, education and engagement. Crowdspot is based in Melbourne.

7. Who are the Victorian Greens? 

The Victorian Greens are a political party with members in the State Parliament. Our MPs represent all of greater Melbourne and also focus on regional issues within Victoria. We work hard for better public transport and V/Line services because we know they’re important to keep our city and state liveable.

8. What if the website isn’t working properly?

If you’re having any issues with the website, please email us at transporttroubles@vic.greens.org.au
