What do the Spots mean?

Strategy or Plan - Any strategic or action oriented document that either relates to or references coastal issues at either a local, regional or higher scale. Generally framing documents providing major directions and including action statements. These may include coastal management plans, coastal landscape plans, climate change strategies, scoping studies etc.

Spatial Data (GIS) - Information that currently resides in a GIS system usually as a layer or as a spreadsheet providing the specific location of assets, features, points of interest, socio economic information etc.

Image - Generally photos either current or historic that identify a coastal location in Port Phillip. It may show current conditions, historic aerial images, general photos of the coast, beach profile outlines, marine assets, vegetation and other graphical information. You can also add an image of the cover of a strategy or plan as well.

Technical Report - These include guidelines and guides, specific investigations, engineering analysis, detailed coastal processes documents and modelling. Often these include other data including GIS layers, spreadsheets and images/graphs.

Other - If you can’t decide from any of these options, because it might be a combination of report/GIS database or image/GIS database, or you are just not sure use the “Other” Spot.
