Frequently asked questions

1. Why is RMIT developing an Integrated Sustainable Transport Strategy?

RMIT is commitment to transforming itself into an organisation that models institution-wide excellence in response to national and international calls for tertiary education to play an influential role in helping shape a sustainable future. RMIT acknowledges that its identity and location, as well as its teaching and learning and research activities afford a particular sphere of influence that predisposes RMIT as a significant force to advance the sustainability agenda.

2. What happens to my feedback?

All of the feedback provided will inform the development of a set of key actions to enable and motivate staff and students to make more sustainable travel choices. However, not all of the issues will be within the University’s direct influence (e.g. bicycle infrastructure like separated lanes). This feedback will help RMIT to advocate to the relevant authorities for improvements that will make sustainable travel easier for staff and students.

3. How long will the map be open for?

The map will be open for contribution until 2 June 2014.

4. How will my personal information be used?

The personal information requested on this form is being collected by RMIT and will be properly safeguarded and processed in accordance with the requirements of the Information Privacy Act (2000).

5. Do you delete any contributions?

All spots are subject to moderation from time to time to check for inappropriate content that is either inappropriate (spots may be edited or omitted). For more information see the Moderation Policy.
